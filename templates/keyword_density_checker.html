Frequently Asked Questions

What is keyword density?

Keyword density refers to the percentage of times a keyword or phrase appears in a text compared to the total number of words. It's a metric used in SEO to analyze how frequently a target keyword appears in content.

What is the ideal keyword density for SEO?

There's no perfect keyword density percentage, but most SEO experts recommend keeping your primary keyword density between 1-2%. Using a keyword too frequently (keyword stuffing) can negatively impact your SEO, while too infrequent usage might not signal relevance to search engines.

Why should I check my content's keyword density?

Checking keyword density helps ensure your content is optimized for search engines without crossing into keyword stuffing territory. It also helps identify which keywords you're naturally using most frequently, which can inform your SEO strategy.

Does this tool exclude common words like "the" and "and"?

Yes, our tool filters out common stop words (like "the," "and," "is," etc.) that don't add significant meaning to your content, allowing you to focus on the keywords that matter for SEO.

How can I use the phrase analysis for SEO?

The phrase analysis helps you identify potential long-tail keywords in your content. These multi-word phrases often have less competition in search results and can help you target more specific search queries related to your topic.
