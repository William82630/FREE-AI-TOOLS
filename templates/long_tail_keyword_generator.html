Frequently Asked Questions

What are long tail keywords?

Long tail keywords are longer, more specific keyword phrases that visitors are more likely to use when they're closer to making a purchase or when they're using voice search. They're typically 3-5 words or more and are more specific than generic "head" keywords.

Why should I use long tail keywords?

Long tail keywords typically have less competition, making them easier to rank for in search results. They also tend to have higher conversion rates because they're more specific and often indicate a clearer user intent. Additionally, they help you capture traffic from more specific searches that might be missed with broader keywords.

How many long tail keywords should I target in my content?

There's no fixed number, but it's generally better to focus on a primary long tail keyword and a few related variations per piece of content. This allows you to create focused content that thoroughly addresses a specific topic rather than trying to cover too many different keywords at once.

What's the difference between informational and commercial keywords?

Informational keywords indicate that the user is looking for information or answers (e.g., "how to optimize website speed"). Commercial keywords suggest that the user has purchase intent or is considering a purchase (e.g., "best SEO tools for small business"). Different types of keywords are useful for different stages of your marketing funnel.

How accurate are the generated keywords?

Our tool generates keywords based on AI analysis of search patterns and semantic relationships. While the keywords are designed to be relevant and natural, we recommend verifying their search volume and competition using dedicated SEO tools before building your strategy around them.
