Frequently Asked Questions

How many tags should I use for my YouTube video?

While YouTube allows up to 500 characters for tags, it's generally recommended to use 5-15 highly relevant tags rather than stuffing your video with every possible keyword. Focus on quality over quantity, starting with the most specific tags that directly describe your content, followed by more general category tags.

Do YouTube tags still matter for SEO?

Yes, tags still play a role in YouTube SEO, although their importance has somewhat diminished compared to title, description, and content relevance. Tags help YouTube understand the context of your video and can help your content appear in related videos sections. They're especially useful for less established channels and for targeting specific search terms.

Should I include my competitors' names in my tags?

It's generally not recommended to use competitor brand names or channel names as tags unless your video is legitimately about them. This practice can be seen as misleading and may violate YouTube's policies. Focus instead on relevant keywords that accurately describe your content.

How often should I update my YouTube tags?

It's a good practice to review and update your tags periodically, especially if you notice changes in search trends or if your video isn't performing as well as expected. For evergreen content, quarterly reviews may be sufficient, while trending content might benefit from more frequent updates.

Do longer tags work better than shorter ones?

Both have their place in an optimized tag strategy. Shorter tags (1-2 words) often have higher search volume but also higher competition. Longer, more specific tags (3+ words) typically have lower search volume but can be easier to rank for and often have higher conversion rates as they match more specific search intent.
